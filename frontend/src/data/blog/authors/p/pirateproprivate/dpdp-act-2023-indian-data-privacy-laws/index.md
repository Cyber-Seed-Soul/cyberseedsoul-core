# DPDP Act, 2023

<p style="text-align: justify;">
No matter if you're a student coding in a college hostel, a farmer in a village, or any other Indian citizen, it's important to understand these laws and always be ready to question things critically, just like Raghav Chadha in the Indian Assembly.
</p> 

1. As an Indian Citizen, you can withdraw consent for any of your data at any time. You have the absolute right to demand that any company show your data, correct it, or completely delete your personal details from their systems.

2. For non-compliance or failure to maintain reasonable security safeguards, companies face fines of up to 250 crores.

3. If the company doesn’t notify us of data violations and breaches, the penalty is higher and can reach up to 200 crores.

4. If a company messes with your data, you don’t need to run around government offices. You can file a complaint online through the new Digital Data Protection Board.

5. Service Providers, Intermediaries, Data Centers, Body Corporates, and Government Organizations must report any data breach or violation to CERT-In (Indian Computer Emergency Response Team) within 6 hours.

6. The ABDM Health Data Management Policy sets minimum standards for data privacy, security, and interoperability in India's digital health ecosystem. The 2011 SPDI Rules also apply to health data.

7. The RBI regulates financial cybersecurity, while your CIBIL Score and related data fall under the CICRA Act 2005.

8. SEBI Guidelines enforce strict cybersecurity and cyber resilience frameworks for stockbrokers, depository participants, mutual funds, and asset management companies. This is supported by the Companies Act, 2013, and LODR regulations.

9. Security standards for government entities and their private contractors are set by the NISPG Guidelines issued by the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA).

10. NCIIPC oversees the energy sector, while the IT Act, 2000, addresses consumer protection.

<p style="text-align: justify">
India should focus on keeping data within India. This will prevent foreign governments from exploiting citizens' data, training their AI on it, or gaining an advantage by analyzing it. We also face zero-day attacks from foreign countries.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
We have already lost 100 billion dollars to the cybercrime epidemic. On top of that, the Indian Government emphasizes Section 69 and SIM card binding, focusing on controlling data within India, rather than on data sovereignty laws and regulations. Simple SMS OTPs are easily hackable. Shift to using authenticator apps. Actively question the content and apps you consume. We did not study engineering and sales to harm ourselves or spy on our own activities. We did not build AI to diminish our autonomy. Always be careful with the data you provide to AI.
</p>

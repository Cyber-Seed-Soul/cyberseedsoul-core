# The Bureaucratic Mindset of Indian Govt Websites

<p style="text-align: justify">
There is a famous concept in software engineering called Conway’s Law, which states that organizations design systems that mirror their own internal communication structures. If a physical Indian government office is chaotic, requires you to visit five different windows, get three different stamps, and look at massive portraits of politicians on the wall, the website will be built the exact same way.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify">
L1 Tender - Lowest Bidder procurement system(Most of the time, Tenders are given to Thugs, Gangsters, Rowdy boys in India.)

For the majority of the static sites, we don't even need the L1 Tender. Website hosting is free, literally. GitHub Pages is free and open source, and they also provide support for all types of organizations, including government organizations. If the government doesn't want to go down that route, it can create its own homelab. Literally, teenagers are doing homelabbing nowadays, leaving big tech.
</p>

## How to Fix

1. Every website should have a giant, clean search bar and buttons that say "Apply for Driving License" or "Pay Challan."

2. use a single, universally recognized Globe icon in the corner that opens a clean, well-designed modal overlay with language options.

3. Guide the user through simple, conversational steps. Ask one question at a time
   (e.g., "What do you need help with today?" -> "Taxes" -> "Income Tax")

4. Host civic hackathons to redesign the worst government websites. Ivite UI/UX designers and frontend developers   to build Open-Source Prototypes.

5. Tech communities can partner with legal experts to conduct formal UX and accessibility audits on critical government portals. Record all legal violations and File the Public Interest Litigations.

6. Kill the L1 Lowest Bidder Tender.Shift to Quality and Cost-Based Selection.

<p style="text-align: justify">
Google Stitch is a phenomenal, AI-powered "vibe design" tool where you can type a prompt or upload a napkin sketch, and Gemini will instantly generate a beautiful, responsive UI and export the production-ready design.

We need public protests before the government offices using AI Voice prompts to build the government websites,so that the sample work is demonstrated. 
</p>

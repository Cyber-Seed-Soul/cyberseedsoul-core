# Quick Response Codes Used in Quishing
QR Codes store data, which can be anything, such as an image, text, or malicious code.

<p style="text-align: justify;">
In India, everyone uses UPI or similar-looking, similarly functional payments. You can see publicly displayed QR codes and people scanning them, which is very dangerous, but people do it blindly and effortlessly, assuming India is still in “Satya Yugam”. The Reality is that most Indian citizens don’t have civic sense and are rife with hunger and scams, from the streets to the Indian assembly.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
In India, everyone sells customer data, from xerox shops to big companies. Heck, they don’t even need to sell; the personal data will be lying around in shops, streets, and all over the place, so anyone can find your name, mobile number, address, Aadhaar card number, etc.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
Anyone who doesn’t have money on hand can pick up this personal data and start sending money requests, since the UPI ID is very easy: it’s your mobile number@yourbank.  Basically, these scammers, once they have the phone number, will send SMS and MMS, send phishing links, call the person, and track them. You can think of all kinds of emergency situations; they will tell the victims and humanize the situation. Of course, a victim can fall prey to any of these, one single mistake, and the victim is done. Even if you open the SMS, click the phishing link, fall for the emergency situation, or make any of the mistakes, the victim is forever in their database. There are legitimate companies that hire these scammers and set monetary targets.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
In India, people don’t know that scanning a QR Code is a very, very dangerous thing to do, because the QR code scan will open all kinds of ports in your network, and take all the information on your phone within minutes, and also, now the victim's phone is installed with malware and spyware. They may wonder, their internet is slow, how come anyone can access all the information within minutes? Probably, they never heard of network ports, command-line networking, operating system kernels, zero-day attacks, etc.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
Anyone can create a QR code with a computer and a tool like qrencode (in Kali Linux) or QRGen. There are a million ways to generate and send the money to a false bank account when the victim scans.
</p>

1. Please think before you scan the QR code. Ask yourself, are there any alternative ways I could do this, instead of scanning the QR Code? Always check the URL preview in the app or on the website, or read the entire URL. If you can’t read it or if it is an encrypted URL, definitely don’t go for it.

2. Check for any tampering with the QR code. Check for any overlaps on the QR Code.


3. Always keep your operating system up to date, whether on mobile or desktop. Look for each setting in the privacy and security section. 

4. Try using fewer applications to reduce your mobile phone's attack surface.

5. Never enter any sensitive information when scanning the QR Code.

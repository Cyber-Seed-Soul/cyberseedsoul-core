# India's toxic loop

<p style="text-align: justify;">
India's political parties, politicians, governments, and private organizations continuously manipulate the mindset of citizens, ensuring they remain distracted and entrapped in psychological games from childhood through adulthood.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
The typical environment a child is born into in India is not filled with awe and curiosity about the wonders of the universe, Biology, engineering, Mathematical and physical paradoxes, and Financial and Relationship knowledge.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
A child is born into ideologies, identity, Religion, caste, foreign forces, and very rigid beliefs about everything you can think of. Most of the Indian assembly, from the central to the local level, is filled with these ideologies rather than curiosity and problem-solving. In fact, most people don't even know how to identify the problem that they currently have without biases and rigid beliefs. Most of them don't know what a win-win game is(a non-zero-sum game). They always think in primordial logics, and they fight personally and politically, and people love it. This is how a glorious Roman Empire fell.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
As a result, the child picks up these ideologies, grows into adulthood, and makes them more powerful, and this loop continues, even among scientists and educated people. Even in the so-called educated communities in southern states of India, the vote is based on these ideologies, not on education and contribution to society. That is why in Andhra Pradesh, an IAS officer like Jayaprakash Narayan won't get any votes at all.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
Consequently, most of us can't think much. We have to be fixated on some ideology instead of basing our decisions on the Data and Context. It is not our fault alone; the environment is very harsh, rude, and aggressive 24/7. We want to focus on something like capitalism, western ideology, communism, a religion, a caste, or some kind of business ideology. We can't separate a person from his thoughts, his actions, and his context. We go after a person, a god, or some rigid identity we can blame and then move on with our lives. We can't hold multiple conflicts in memory and perform analysis based on them. We can only change the environment by first identifying the environment we are in. Then we can try to change the environment by following best practices or at least be smart about what we can control and what we cannot.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
With all of this in mind, to build a nation, the Indian assembly should include civil engineers, software engineers, nationalists, human philosophers, neuroscientists, economists,  and all other types of engineers and scientists from various engineering fields and subfields, not just movie actors, ideologists, rowdy boys, and girls. Therefore, I urge citizens and policymakers to prioritize electing members with diverse expertise and to reform the selection process. Unless the composition of the assembly changes, India will continue to deteriorate.
</p>
